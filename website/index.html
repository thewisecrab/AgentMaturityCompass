The Problem

Every AI agent claims to be
trustworthy. None can prove it.

Today's AI agents operate on vibes. They say they're safe. They say they're reliable. But when things go wrong — and they will — there's no evidence trail, no audit log, no way to know what happened or why.

🎭

The Trust Theater

"Our AI is safe" means nothing without evidence. Self-reported safety is like a restaurant grading its own hygiene.

🕳️

The Evidence Gap

When an agent makes a bad decision, can you trace why? Most can't. The decision chain is a black box.

📊

The Maturity Illusion

A chatbot and an autonomous trading agent both call themselves "AI agents." They're not the same. Maturity matters.

The Solution

Think credit score,
but for AI agents.

A credit score doesn't ask you if you're good with money — it looks at evidence. AMC does the same for AI agents. Evidence over claims. Math over vibes.

01

Diagnose

111 questions across 5 dimensions probe every aspect of your agent — from how it handles errors to how it protects user data. No hand-waving.

02

Score

25 scoring modules calculate a maturity level from Absent (0) to Optimizing (5). Each score is backed by cryptographic evidence chains — tamper-evident and verifiable.

03

Prove

Every score comes with a Merkle-tree evidence chain. Anyone can verify the assessment independently. No trust required — just math.

The Scale

Six levels of
agent maturity.

Not every agent needs to be Level 5. A personal assistant and an autonomous trading bot have different maturity requirements. AMC helps you understand where you are and where you need to be.

Absent No structure. Reactive. Fragile.
Initial Intent exists but isn't operational.
Developing Partial structure. Works in normal cases.
Defined Repeatable. Measurable. Auditable.
Managed Proactive controls. Risk-calibrated.
Optimizing Self-correcting. Continuously verified.

The Proof

Convention is not enough.
Math is.

Most frameworks rely on self-assessment. AMC uses cryptographic evidence chains — Ed25519 signatures, Merkle trees, and tamper-evident logs. If someone changes a score, the chain breaks. No exceptions.
