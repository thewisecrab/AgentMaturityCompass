EU AI Act Compliance
for AI Agents

High-risk AI systems must demonstrate conformity with Articles 9, 12, 13, 14, and 17 by August 2026. AMC maps your agent's actual behavior to these requirements — with cryptographic proof.

Why Self-Reported Documentation Won't Work

The EU AI Act requires evidence of conformity, not documentation of intent. Every existing framework lets agents grade their own homework. Under the Act, that's explicitly insufficient.

AMC captures agent behavior through a trusted gateway proxy, signs every event with Ed25519 keys, and produces scores from execution evidence — not claims. This is the difference between "we have a policy" and "here's cryptographic proof we enforce it."

Article-by-Article Mapping

  • ✓
    Article 9 — Risk Management
    AMC's 85 assurance packs map to systematic risk identification. Each pack targets specific failure modes with documented mitigation paths.
  • ✓
    Article 12 — Record Keeping
    Merkle-tree evidence ledger with Ed25519 signatures. Every API call, tool use, and decision is hash-chained into a tamper-proof audit trail.
  • ✓
    Article 13 — Transparency
    L0–L5 scores with cryptographic provenance. Auditors can verify scores offline without trusting the operator — using amc verify all --json.
  • ✓
    Article 14 — Human Oversight
    Oversight quality scoring measures when the agent can act alone vs when humans must review. Policy-gated autonomy levels with signed action policies.
  • ✓
    Article 17 — Quality Management
    Fleet consistency scoring and policy canary detection. Continuous monitoring with drift alerts when scores drop below thresholds.

Get Started in 5 Minutes

No account needed. No API key. Free and open source.

$ npm i -g agent-maturity-compass
$ mkdir my-agent && cd my-agent
$ amc init
$ amc quickscore --eu-ai-act
→ L2 (Managed) — ⚠️ IN PROGRESS for EU AI Act
$ amc audit binder create --framework eu-ai-act
→ Compliance evidence exported to .amc/audit/
$ amc compliance report --framework eu-ai-act
→ Gap analysis with per-article remediation steps
