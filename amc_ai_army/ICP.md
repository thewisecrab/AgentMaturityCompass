# Ideal Customer Profiles (ICPs) — AMC Compass Sprint

## ICP 1: Founder-Led B2B Services Firms (10–75 employees)
**Who they are**
- Agencies, consultancies, implementation partners, and outsourced service firms
- Revenue typically $1M–$15M
- Founder/CEO still involved in sales and delivery decisions

**Core pains**
- Delivery quality depends on a few key people
- Margin pressure from labor-heavy execution
- Inconsistent proposal-to-delivery handoffs and SOP adherence

**Why-now pain**
- Growth is stalling because hiring cannot keep pace with demand
- Competitors are using AI-assisted delivery and undercutting timelines/cost
- Key team members are overloaded, creating quality and retention risk

**Qualifying criteria**
- Clear repeatable service workflows (or willingness to standardize)
- Leadership priority to improve utilization, margin, or cycle time in the next 90 days
- At least one operational owner who can implement changes weekly
- Budget and urgency for a sprint-style transformation engagement

## ICP 2: Revenue Teams at Mid-Market SaaS / Tech-Enabled Companies (50–300 employees)
**Who they are**
- B2B SaaS or tech-enabled companies with SDR/AE/CS functions
- Growth-stage teams under pressure to hit aggressive pipeline targets

**Core pains**
- SDR productivity and personalization do not scale
- CRM hygiene is poor, reducing forecast reliability
- Slow lead routing, follow-up, and handoff between GTM teams

**Why-now pain**
- Pipeline coverage is below target for upcoming quarter
- CAC is rising while conversion rates are flat/declining
- Board/investor pressure for efficient growth, not headcount-heavy growth

**Qualifying criteria**
- Existing CRM + sales engagement stack in place
- GTM leader committed to process changes (not only tooling)
- Measurable KPI baseline (meetings booked, conversion rate, cycle length)
- Ability to run controlled pilots with clear ownership and adoption plan

---

## ICP 3: Multi-Location SMB Operators (Franchise / Healthcare / Home Services)
**Who they are**
- Multi-site operators with centralized leadership and distributed frontline teams
- 5–100 locations with recurring customer interactions

**Core pains**
- Inconsistent customer communication and service quality across sites
- High administrative overhead and manual scheduling/follow-up
- Training variance and repeated mistakes at the location level

**Why-now pain**
- Expansion plans are blocked by operational inconsistency
- Labor shortages make process efficiency mandatory
- Customer experience gaps are impacting reviews, renewals, or referrals

**Qualifying criteria**
- Standard workflows that can be templated and rolled out across locations
- Central sponsor with authority across operations
- Location managers willing to adopt shared systems/processes
- Clear business objective tied to speed, consistency, or customer retention

---

## Fast Prioritization Rubric (Across All ICPs)
Prioritize accounts that score high on:
1. **Urgency** (active pain in current quarter)
2. **Repeatability** (workflows can be systematized)
3. **Decision velocity** (sponsor + operator access)
4. **Economic upside** (margin, throughput, conversion, retention impact)
5. **Change readiness** (team can adopt new operating rhythm quickly)
