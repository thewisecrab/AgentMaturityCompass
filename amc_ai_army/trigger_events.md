# High-Intent Buying Signals — AMC Compass Sprint

## How to use this list
Trigger events are strongest when they indicate **urgent operational pain + executive attention + budget window**. Pair each trigger with qualifying questions before advancing.

---

## 1) Leadership or organizational change
**Examples**
- New COO, CRO, Head of Operations, or VP Sales hired
- Founder delegates day-to-day execution to new operator

**Why this signals intent**
- New leaders need quick wins and visible process improvements
- They are more open to changing systems and workflows

**Why-now pain**
- Pressure to prove impact in first 90–180 days
- Existing inefficiencies become more visible during transition

**Qualification checks**
- Is this leader chartered to improve efficiency/margins/pipeline now?
- Do they have authority to change process across teams?
- Is there a defined quarterly target they must hit?

---

## 2) Missed growth targets / pipeline shortfall
**Examples**
- Public or internal acknowledgment of missed revenue goals
- Hiring freeze despite growth targets staying constant

**Why this signals intent**
- Teams need output gains without proportional headcount increases
- Efficiency initiatives move from “nice-to-have” to mandatory

**Why-now pain**
- Immediate pressure from board/investors/owners for turnaround
- Existing GTM and delivery bottlenecks are now financially painful

**Qualification checks**
- What target was missed and by how much?
- Is there executive mandate for productivity improvements this quarter?
- Can they commit resources to pilot and adoption?

---

## 3) Rapid hiring, then delivery strain (or hiring slowdown)
**Examples**
- Headcount grew quickly but onboarding/process maturity lags
- Hiring slowed, yet workload remains high

**Why this signals intent**
- Process debt accumulates and quality drops under growth stress
- Automation and standardization become critical to maintain output

**Why-now pain**
- Teams are overloaded; error rates and cycle time increase
- Leadership must protect margin and service quality simultaneously

**Qualification checks**
- Are teams reporting rework, delays, or inconsistent quality?
- Are SOPs documented and enforceable today?
- Is there appetite to redesign workflow, not just buy software?

---

## 4) New market expansion or multi-location rollout
**Examples**
- New territory launch, franchise expansion, added service lines
- M&A integration requiring process harmonization

**Why this signals intent**
- Expansion exposes inconsistency and coordination gaps
- Need for scalable operating model becomes immediate

**Why-now pain**
- Inconsistent execution threatens launch outcomes and brand trust
- Management overhead spikes when systems are fragmented

**Qualification checks**
- Is expansion timeline fixed and near-term?
- Are cross-site/process standards currently weak?
- Is a central owner accountable for rollout quality?

---

## 5) Tech stack change (CRM/ERP/Helpdesk revamp)
**Examples**
- CRM migration, sales engagement overhaul, workflow tooling refresh
- Consolidation of point tools to reduce costs

**Why this signals intent**
- Tool changes create a natural window for process redesign
- Decision-makers are already engaged in operational architecture

**Why-now pain**
- Migration risk is high if legacy processes are broken
- Teams fear disruption unless workflows are simplified and standardized

**Qualification checks**
- Is process redesign in scope, or only system migration?
- Who owns adoption and change management?
- Are success metrics defined beyond go-live completion?

---

## 6) Margin compression / rising delivery cost
**Examples**
- Gross margin decline over consecutive quarters
- Increased labor cost without equivalent pricing power

**Why this signals intent**
- Financial pressure creates urgency for throughput and utilization gains
- Leaders become more receptive to short-cycle, ROI-first initiatives

**Why-now pain**
- Current operating model is no longer economically viable
- Delayed action compounds losses and burnout

**Qualification checks**
- Is margin recovery a top-3 executive priority?
- Are they tracking unit economics by workflow/team?
- Can they tie improvements to measurable financial outcomes?

---

## 7) Customer experience deterioration (NPS/CSAT/reviews)
**Examples**
- Increase in complaints, churn risk, or negative public reviews
- Slower response times and missed SLAs

**Why this signals intent**
- CX decline is a leading indicator of process and capacity breakdown
- Revenue retention risk accelerates buying decisions

**Why-now pain**
- Renewals, referrals, and reputation are actively threatened
- Frontline teams are firefighting instead of executing predictably

**Qualification checks**
- Which customer metrics are deteriorating and at what rate?
- Is leadership treating this as a strategic issue?
- Is there cross-functional ownership of the fix?

---

## 8) Compliance, audit, or documentation pressure
**Examples**
- Upcoming audit, industry compliance updates, client security reviews
- Requirement for better process traceability/documentation

**Why this signals intent**
- Manual, inconsistent workflows become risky and expensive
- Standardized systems and repeatable controls become non-negotiable

**Why-now pain**
- Regulatory/customer deadlines create hard timelines
- Non-compliance risk carries financial and reputational penalties

**Qualification checks**
- What deadline or audit event is driving urgency?
- Which workflows currently lack traceability?
- Is there budget approved for risk reduction initiatives?

---

## Buying-Signal Scoring (Quick Triage)
Score each account 1–5 on:
1. **Trigger strength** (material event with executive visibility)
2. **Time pressure** (deadline inside 3–6 months)
3. **Economic impact** (clear upside/downside in dollars)
4. **Change authority** (access to sponsor + implementation owner)
5. **Operational readiness** (baseline metrics + process ownership)

**Prioritize outreach when total score ≥18/25.**

---

## Discovery Questions to Confirm High Intent
- What changed recently that makes this a priority now?
- What happens if this is not solved this quarter?
- Which KPI is under the most pressure today?
- Who owns delivery of the outcome (not just purchase decision)?
- What is the decision and rollout timeline?
