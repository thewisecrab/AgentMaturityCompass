# Qualification Framework for Founder-Led $5k Sprint Sales

Use this sheet during/after discovery to decide fast: **Advance, Nurture, or Disqualify**.

---

## 1) Qualification Model: PACT-DO

Score each area 0–2. Max = 12.

- **P — Pain**: Is the problem urgent and costly?
- **A — Authority**: Is the decision-maker involved?
- **C — Capacity (Budget)**: Can they spend $5k now?
- **T — Timeline**: Can they start within 1–2 weeks?
- **D — Definition**: Is the sprint outcome specific/measurable?
- **O — Owner**: Is there a single client-side owner for execution?

### Score Interpretation
- **10–12**: Advance to proposal + close call immediately
- **7–9**: Conditional advance (resolve blockers in 24–48h)
- **0–6**: Nurture or disqualify

---

## 2) Mandatory Gate (Non-Negotiables)
All must be true to close:
- Decision-maker signs off (directly or present)
- Budget exists for $5k sprint
- Start date within 14 days
- One clear business outcome

If any missing → do not push close.

---

## 3) Discovery Questions by Qualification Dimension

## Pain
- "What is the core issue right now?"
- "What is this costing you weekly?"
- "What happens if this remains unsolved next 30 days?"

## Authority
- "Who needs to approve this?"
- "Will they join the next call?"

## Capacity (Budget)
- "Do you have budget allocated to solve this now?"
- "Are you comfortable investing $5k if scope and outcomes are clear?"

## Timeline
- "When do you need this solved by?"
- "If we align this week, can we start next week?"

## Definition
- "What exact outcome would make this sprint successful?"
- "Which metric or milestone should move?"

## Owner
- "Who on your team will own communication and approvals during sprint?"

## 5) Conditional Advance Rules
You may advance conditionally only if:
- Pain and timeline are strong, and
- One major blocker can be resolved within 48h

Examples:
- Need final CFO confirmation
- Need internal owner named
- Need exact KPI finalized

If blocker unresolved in 48h, move to nurture.

---

## 6) Disqualification Triggers
Disqualify when any of these patterns appear:
- No real urgency; "sometime later"
- No clear economic impact
- No path to decision-maker
- Budget fantasy ("maybe next quarter")
- Scope is vague and keeps expanding pre-commitment
- Prospect seeks free consulting without intent to buy

Use respectful close:
"I don’t think a sprint is the right fit right now. Better to pause and revisit when [budget/priority/owner] is in place."

---

## 7) Close Readiness Checklist
Before asking for commitment, confirm aloud:
- "We’re solving [problem]"
- "Success means [measurable outcome]"
- "You can decide this week"
- "You can start by [date]"
- "[Owner] will run point internally"
- "Fee is fixed at $5,000"

Then ask:
"Are you comfortable moving forward if I send the final sprint plan now?"

---

## 8) Fast Decision SLA
- Discovery complete → proposal within 2 hours
- Proposal sent → decision request within 24h
- No decision after 72h → close-lost + recycle

This keeps momentum high and avoids pipeline drag.
