# AMC Pricing Model (Sales Playbook Version)

## 0) Use this file in live sales conversations
- Keep it practical, plain-language, and objection-ready.
- All prices are USD.
- Assumption baseline (update if operating costs change):
  - Delivery team blended direct cost for a standard Sprint: **~$2,200**
  - Minimum accepted gross margin for any new deal: **50%**

---

## 1) Compass Sprint ($5k)

### What is the offer
Fast diagnostic + execution-ready plan for the next 30–90 days.

### Time estimate (hours)
- **Internal delivery estimate:** **~44 hours** total
  - 1) Discovery + stakeholder alignment: **6h**
  - 2) Evidence intake + evidence quality checks: **8h**
  - 3) Scoring + gap analysis + risk scoring: **12h**
  - 4) 30/60/90 roadmap + initiative prioritization: **12h**
  - 5) Executive summary + handoff meeting: **4h**
  - 6) Communication/admin/rework buffer: **2h**

### Cost estimate
- **Delivery cost estimate:** ~$2,200
- **Fee:** $5,000

### Gross margin (target)
- Gross margin = (5,000 - 2,200) / 5,000 = **56%**
- **Gross margin target band:** **55–60%** (depending delivery load)

### What is included
- 5 business day engagement window
- One named implementation owner and sponsor alignment
- Maturity baseline + scorecard for current agent operations
- Evidence-backed gap findings mapped to risks and owners
- Prioritized 30/60/90-day roadmap (impact / confidence / effort)
- Executive decision memo + meeting to confirm top 3 decisions
- One revision pass for clarity within 5 business days

### What is **not** included
- Production implementation/build of new agents or infrastructure
- Full-day support, managed operations, or ongoing triage
- Additional tools/custom integrations outside current stack baseline
- Legal compliance attestations or external audits
- Additional KPI instrumentation beyond agreed baseline

### Quick sales framing
"If you need a clear, defensible baseline fast, start here. You get clarity first, then choose build scope only where scorecard risk justifies spend."

## 2) Retainer tiers (monthly)

### Retainer A — **$3,000/mo** (Foundation)
**For:** one team, one operational domain, early governance standardization

**Deliverables / cadence**
- Monthly reassessment and score refresh
- 1 weekly async health check + 1 monthly leadership sync (45 min)
- 4 remediation tracking updates + blocker calls
- Quarterly strategy note + roadmap correction

**Scope**
- Up to **2 agent systems/workflows** in scope
- Up to **1 executive** + **1 operational** reporting dashboard
- New onboarding tasks for one additional system only with prior review

---

### Retainer B — **$7,000/mo** (Growth)
**For:** 2–3 teams, multiple workflows, recurring execution pressure

**Deliverables / cadence**
- Bi-weekly operations sync + monthly executive steering (60 min)
- 2 quarterly decision packages (maturity trend + top investment picks)
- Incident/risk watch and remediation review on priority workflows
- Monthly execution checklist with status by owner

**Scope**
- Up to **5 systems/workflows** and cross-functional reporting
- KPI baselining + trend tracking across core workflows
- Up to **2 new workflow onboarding events** per month with scope approval

---

### Retainer C — **$15,000/mo** (Enterprise/Scale)
**For:** rapid expansion risk, governance + reliability + board-ready reporting requirements

**Deliverables / cadence**
- Weekly ops sync + monthly executive board-ready review
- Continuous readiness monitoring + drift alerts on critical controls
- Quarterly executive package: maturity delta, risk posture, ROI estimate, roadmap reprioritization
- Priority roadmap delivery support (advisory + coaching cadence)

**Scope**
- Up to **10 systems/workflows** in managed scope
- Formal remediation governance (owners, review windows, escalation policy)
- Priority triage support and decision support for scaling and new product launches

## 3) Rapid Pilot ($12k)

### Scope
A speed-to-impact implementation engagement focused on one urgent, high-priority use case:
- 14-day timeline
- One production workflow fully scoped, built, tested, and shipped
- KPI baseline defined in proposal + post-pilot delta review
- Operational controls, escalation path, and handoff/runbook

### How it differs from Compass Sprint
- **Sprint = diagnose + prioritize**
- **Pilot = diagnose + ship one use case**
- Pilot includes technical execution, testing, and measurable KPI uplift; Sprint excludes implementation
- Pilot has higher fee and longer schedule because it includes build risk and rollback/testing overhead

### Typical use when to propose
- Team has clear owner and data access for one use case
- Wants quick measurable proof before wider rollout
- Has budget for short-interval implementation and is ready to act in 2 weeks

## 4) Discounting policy

**Principle:** Use concessions to reduce risk, not to train price resistance.

- **Default:** No discount
- **When discounts are allowed:**
  1. Strategic account with unusually long cycle, but needs a firm commitment to start and decide
  2. Procurement pressure with clear internal approval barrier
  3. Multiple future scope opportunities (pipeline-linked)

- **Maximum discount by approval level:**
  - Up to **5%**: AE + RevOps/Rev Manager approval
  - **6–10%**: requires **REV_CFO_FINANCE** + **REV_HEAD_OF_SALES** sign-off
  - Above **10%**: **Founder/COO-level approval only** and written justification

- **Hard rule (minimum margin floor):** final offer must keep **≥50% gross margin** after all concessions.
  - If discount math breaks margin floor, reject discount and instead propose
    - scope narrowing, or
    - payment timing flexibility, or
    - path downgrade (Pilot → Sprint)

## 5) Price anchoring strategy (maximize Sprint conversion)

Use a **Good/Better/Best + risk ladder** so the $5k choice looks like the best entry decision:

1. **Good (Sprint, $5k)**
   - "Fastest way to reduce uncertainty"
   - fixed scope, fixed timeline, clear decision output

2. **Better (Pilot, $12k)**
   - same framework + actual one-use-case implementation
   - higher time/implementation commitment, lower if they already know where they want to start

3. **Best (Retainer)**
   - for teams ready to operationalize continuously, not one-off clarity

### Sales language (high-converting pattern)
1. State outcome pain + cost of delay.
2. Anchor with Sprint as the low-risk way to unlock facts and a ranked plan.
3. Introduce Pilot only if they have one clear owner/use case.
4. If they argue “too expensive,” anchor to decision cost:
   - "The bigger risk is spending unplanned weeks on unprioritized fixes. Sprint gives you a defensible map first."
5. Put decision date and start date in the same sentence: **"Sprint lock-in this week, kickoff in 48h."**

## 6) Scope creep triggers and renegotiation protocol

### What triggers renegotiation
- Adding additional systems/workflows not in approved offer
- New KPI tracking requirements across extra domains
- Extra deliverables (custom dashboards, legal/compliance artifacts, full implementation of additional workflows)
- More support hours than cadence allows
- New stakeholder requests that change decision ownership or governance model

### How to handle in conversation
1. **Pause and document**: confirm original scope was completed.
2. **Quantify addition**: estimate hours/cost/risk for each add-on.
3. **Choose response option**:
   - **Out of sprint/retainer scope** → issue Phase 2 scope addendum with delta.
   - **Within urgent safety exception** → include as separate line item with timeline + fee adjustment.
   - **Low effort** → include only if it does not breach margin floor and timeline.
4. **Ask for written approval**: verbal agreement accepted only if follow-up confirmation sent before work.
5. **Protect baseline**: no work outside scope until signed scope change / CR.
