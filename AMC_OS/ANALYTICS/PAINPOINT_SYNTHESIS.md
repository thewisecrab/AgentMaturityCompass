## Detailed Pain Points (Top 15)

### 1) Agent outputs are unreliable/inconsistent in production
- **Pain statement:** Teams see strong demos but unstable real-world behavior under distribution shift and varied inputs.
- **Evidence basis:** Repeated pattern across GenAI adoption cycles: prototype success with production degradation; reliability is top blocker to enterprise rollout.
- **AMC dimension that addresses it:** Maturity model + continuous eval/observability + release gates.
- **Outreach messaging angle:** “Turn impressive demos into dependable production agents with reliability gates that executives can trust.”

### 2) No standardized eval framework before release
- **Pain statement:** Teams ship without consistent test suites, thresholds, or pass/fail governance.
- **Evidence basis:** Common overreliance on anecdotal QA and manual spot checks in early agent programs.
- **AMC dimension:** Evaluation operating system (test taxonomy, benchmark cadence, quality thresholds).
- **Outreach angle:** “If you can’t measure agent quality consistently, you can’t scale safely—AMC gives you production-grade eval discipline.”

### 3) Governance exists on paper but not in delivery workflow
- **Pain statement:** Policies are documented, but teams bypass them when delivery pressure rises.
- **Evidence basis:** Frequent mismatch between policy docs and sprint-level execution reality.
- **AMC dimension:** Embedded governance workflows and stage gates integrated into operating cadence.
- **Outreach angle:** “Move from policy PDFs to governance that actually runs inside your delivery process.”

### 4) Unclear ownership and decision rights for AI risk
- **Pain statement:** Product, engineering, legal, security, and compliance all influence decisions, but accountability is ambiguous.
- **Evidence basis:** Cross-functional friction is a top cause of delays and inconsistent risk posture.
- **AMC dimension:** Governance RACI, escalation paths, role-based decision frameworks.
- **Outreach angle:** “Stop AI-risk ping-pong—AMC clarifies who decides what, when, and with what evidence.”

### 5) Compliance/audit requests trigger fire drills
- **Pain statement:** Evidence is scattered, so responding to audit/compliance demands is slow and disruptive.
- **Evidence basis:** Common in regulated and maturing enterprises where controls exist but evidence collection is manual.
- **AMC dimension:** Audit-ready control mapping and evidence workflows.
- **Outreach angle:** “Cut audit prep from scramble to system with continuously captured governance evidence.”

### 6) Pilot-to-production stall (“POC graveyard”)
- **Pain statement:** Multiple pilots never graduate due to risk uncertainty and absent operational standards.
- **Evidence basis:** Widely observed in enterprise AI programs with weak transition criteria.
- **AMC dimension:** Maturity progression model and production-readiness checkpoints.
- **Outreach angle:** “Graduate more pilots to production with clear readiness criteria and governance sign-off.”

### 7) Hallucination/safety incidents erode internal trust
- **Pain statement:** One visible failure can freeze investment and tighten approvals.
- **Evidence basis:** High salience of incidents in enterprise decision making; reputational risk sensitivity.
- **AMC dimension:** Safety evals + incident response playbooks + risk severity routing.
- **Outreach angle:** “Reduce incident frequency and recover trust faster with structured safety governance.”

### 8) Fragmented tooling with no unified visibility
- **Pain statement:** Traces, evals, policy docs, and business outcomes live in separate systems.
- **Evidence basis:** Typical stack sprawl across rapid GenAI adoption phases.
- **AMC dimension:** Unified operating layer connecting technical signals to governance and business KPIs.
- **Outreach angle:** “Unify fragmented AI signals into one operating view for delivery leaders and risk owners.”

### 9) Prompt/version changes are unmanaged and risky
- **Pain statement:** Teams tweak prompts/models without controlled release discipline or rollback clarity.
- **Evidence basis:** Prompt iteration velocity often outruns change-management process.
- **AMC dimension:** Change governance, versioning discipline, approval and rollback workflows.
- **Outreach angle:** “Ship agent changes with confidence using controlled version governance and fast rollback paths.”

### 10) Teams cannot quantify ROI of agent initiatives
- **Pain statement:** Leadership sees activity but not attributable business impact.
- **Evidence basis:** Frequent budget scrutiny due to unclear KPI attribution from agent deployments.
- **AMC dimension:** KPI architecture linking reliability/risk metrics to cost, productivity, and revenue outcomes.
- **Outreach angle:** “Translate agent performance into business impact metrics your CFO and COO will trust.”

### 11) Human-in-the-loop design is ad hoc and expensive
- **Pain statement:** Review workflows are either too heavy (slow/costly) or too light (risky).
- **Evidence basis:** Teams struggle to calibrate intervention thresholds and reviewer utilization.
- **AMC dimension:** HITL operating design by risk tier and task criticality.
- **Outreach angle:** “Right-size human oversight to reduce risk without crushing throughput.”

### 12) Security/privacy concerns block broader rollout
- **Pain statement:** Data handling uncertainty and access-control gaps stall deployment approvals.
- **Evidence basis:** Security/legal checkpoints often become late-stage blockers.
- **AMC dimension:** Governance-by-design with policy controls, data boundary definitions, and security review gates.
- **Outreach angle:** “Accelerate approvals by embedding security/privacy governance from day one.”

### 13) Business stakeholders cannot interpret technical metrics
- **Pain statement:** Engineering dashboards don’t answer executive risk/value questions.
- **Evidence basis:** Common communication gap between ML/engineering teams and business leadership.
- **AMC dimension:** Executive-readable scorecards and maturity reporting.
- **Outreach angle:** “Bridge technical depth and executive clarity with decision-ready AI governance reporting.”

### 14) Scaling across business units creates policy drift
- **Pain statement:** Each team adopts AI differently, creating inconsistent controls and quality standards.
- **Evidence basis:** Decentralized adoption patterns increase variance and governance drift.
- **AMC dimension:** Federated governance model with shared standards and local autonomy guardrails.
- **Outreach angle:** “Scale AI across teams without losing control—standardize what matters, flex what doesn’t.”

### 15) Vendor dependence creates lock-in and architecture anxiety
- **Pain statement:** Teams fear dependence on one framework/model stack and future switching costs.
- **Evidence basis:** Rapid vendor evolution drives procurement and architecture caution.
- **AMC dimension:** Model/tool-agnostic governance and portability-minded operating standards.
- **Outreach angle:** “Future-proof your AI governance so strategy survives model and tooling shifts.”
