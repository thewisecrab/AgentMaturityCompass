## Journey Scope
First touchpoint (ad/post/outreach) → Awareness → Qualification → Proposal → Signature → Pre-sprint Onboarding → Day 1 Sprint Kickoff

---

## STAGE 1 — FIRST TOUCHPOINT (Ad / Post / Cold Outreach)

### What Happens
Prospect sees a LinkedIn post, outbound email, or referral mention of AMC.

### Friction Points
| # | Friction | Root Cause |
|---|----------|------------|
| 1.1 | Unclear "what is this?" — no immediate clarity on what AMC delivers vs. generic "AI consulting" | Undifferentiated category language |
| 1.2 | No proof of relevance — prospect can't self-identify as ICP in 5 seconds | Missing ICP signals in headline |
| 1.3 | CTA asks for high commitment (book a call) before trust is established | First-touchpoint commitment mismatch |

### Drop-Off Risk: **HIGH**
Most people leave without a second interaction. Zero brand recognition compounds this.

### Friction Reduction Ideas
- Add "Who this is for" bullet in every post/outreach (AI-first teams, 10–200 people, deploying agents now)
- Offer a lower-commitment CTA: "Get sample scorecard" or "See what we assess" instead of "Book a call"
- Use specificity as proof: "We assess 7 AI maturity dimensions across 40+ controls" outperforms "AI strategy advice"

---

## STAGE 2 — LANDING PAGE / CONTENT (Consideration)

### What Happens
Prospect clicks through to LP or content asset. Evaluates whether to engage further.

### Friction Points
| # | Friction | Root Cause |
|---|----------|------------|
| 2.1 | Price not visible; prospect has to book a call to find out cost | Pricing opacity → qualification friction |
| 2.2 | Deliverables are abstract ("roadmap", "scorecard") — no sample output visible | Lack of tangible proof |
| 2.3 | "5 business days" timeline raises doubt: "How can you assess my company in 5 days?" | Credibility gap on speed |
| 2.4 | No social proof from recognizable ICP peers | Trust deficit for unknown brand |
| 2.5 | Single CTA (book a call) — too high-commitment for first visit | CTA commitment mismatch |

### Drop-Off Risk: **HIGH**
Majority of visitors don't convert on first visit without enough signal to trust and self-qualify.

### Friction Reduction Ideas
- Show $5,000 price on the LP; anchor it against cost-of-delay (see P3 experiment)
- Publish a redacted sample scorecard or one-page illustrative output
- Add a "How it works in 5 days" visual timeline: Day 1 kickoff → Day 5 executive readout
- Add a "Who this is NOT for" section — increases trust by demonstrating selectivity
- Add dual CTA: primary "Book Diagnostic Call" + secondary "Download Sample Assessment"

---

## STAGE 3 — DIAGNOSTIC CALL (Qualification)

### What Happens
Prospect books and joins a 20-min diagnostic call. AMC qualifies and pitches.

### Friction Points
| # | Friction | Root Cause |
|---|----------|------------|
| 3.1 | Booking link friction — extra steps, time zones, unavailable slots | Calendar tool friction |
| 3.2 | No pre-call prep sent to prospect — they come in cold | Missing pre-call enablement |
| 3.3 | Prospect doesn't bring the right stakeholder (individual contributor vs. decision-maker) | Unclear who should attend |
| 3.4 | Call feels like generic discovery, not a "mini-diagnostic" that creates value | No value delivery in the call itself |
| 3.5 | No clear "what happens next" at end of call — prospect leaves in ambiguity | Missing close sequence |

### Drop-Off Risk: **MEDIUM-HIGH**
No-shows and mis-qualified attendees waste capacity; ambiguous next steps lose warm prospects.

### Friction Reduction Ideas
- Send 3-question pre-call intake form (what AI systems deployed, biggest concern, who makes the decision)
- Use pre-call email: "Here's what we'll cover + please bring your AI ops lead or CTO"
- Run first 10 minutes of call as a "mini-diagnostic" — ask questions that make prospect feel assessed, not pitched
- End every call with a written "decision memo" (1 page, sent within 2 hours): what was diagnosed, what the Sprint would address, what happens if they don't act

## STAGE 4 — PROPOSAL & SOW RECEIPT

### What Happens
AMC sends proposal/SOW. Prospect reviews, potentially negotiates, and signs.

### Friction Points
| # | Friction | Root Cause |
|---|----------|------------|
| 4.1 | Proposal arrives >24h after call — momentum lost | Slow turnaround |
| 4.2 | Proposal too long or legalistic — prospect doesn't read it | Format mismatch |
| 4.3 | Acceptance criteria are vague — buyer uncertainty about what "done" means | Scope ambiguity |
| 4.4 | Payment/contract process requires manual back-and-forth | No e-signature / payment automation |
| 4.5 | No explicit decision deadline in proposal | Deals stall indefinitely |

### Drop-Off Risk: **MEDIUM**
Deals that stall here often die quietly. Speed and clarity are the primary levers.

### Friction Reduction Ideas
- Issue proposal within 2 hours of call (use PROPOSAL_TEMPLATE.md + pre-fill discovery fields)
- Use a 1-page visual "sprint brief" as the first attachment, with full SOW as appendix
- Add explicit acceptance criteria: "Sprint is complete when [readout deck delivered + client sign-off received]"
- Implement e-signature + Stripe/payment link in same email; minimize click count to commit
- Include decision deadline: "This start slot is available if confirmed by [date + 3 business days]"

## STAGE 5 — SIGNATURE TO PRE-SPRINT GATE (Day 0)

### What Happens
Deal is signed. AMC prepares. Client must complete onboarding checklist before Day 1 begins.

### Friction Points
| # | Friction | Root Cause |
|---|----------|------------|
| 5.1 | Client doesn't know what to prepare — onboarding checklist arrives late or unclear | No immediate welcome + prep sequence |
| 5.2 | Evidence collection is overwhelming — clients don't know where to start | Too many items, no prioritization |
| 5.3 | Wrong stakeholders identified — SMEs blocked or unavailable for Day 2 interviews | Stakeholder map not collected upfront |
| 5.4 | Access provisioning (workspace, shared folder) creates delays | Manual provisioning, no automation |
| 5.5 | No sense of "this is happening" — excitement drops between signature and kickoff | Emotional momentum gap |

### Drop-Off Risk: **MEDIUM** (deal is signed, but sprint quality degrades if gate fails)
Gate failures delay sprint start; delayed sprints risk client dissatisfaction and retainer-attach failure.

### Friction Reduction Ideas
- Send automated "Welcome + Sprint Prep Kit" within 30 minutes of signature (email + shared folder link)
- Prioritize evidence checklist: "Top 5 items that unlock 80% of our assessment" — don't overwhelm with 20+ items
- Stakeholder interview slots: collect names + calendar availability in signature follow-up
- Pre-build client workspace folder structure; client just drops files into pre-labeled folders
- Send a "Day 1 preview" email: "Here's what your first morning will look like on [date]" — builds confidence

---

## STAGE 6 — DAY 1 KICKOFF

### What Happens
Sprint begins. Kickoff call, evidence review, interview scheduling.

### Friction Points
| # | Friction | Root Cause |
|---|----------|------------|
| 6.1 | Kickoff call has wrong attendees — decision-maker absent | Not confirmed in Stage 5 |
| 6.2 | Evidence is incomplete — AMC cannot start scoring | Stage 5 checklist incomplete |
| 6.3 | Client unclear on what they'll receive — readout format surprise | Expectation mismatch |
| 6.4 | Timezone/scheduling issues for interview block | Not locked pre-sprint |
| 6.5 | Technical friction: screen sharing, tool access, file permissions | Not pre-tested |

### Drop-Off Risk: **LOW** (paid client) but **HIGH impact on satisfaction**
A rocky Day 1 sets the tone for the entire sprint and directly impacts retainer attach.

### Friction Reduction Ideas
- Run pre-sprint "tech check" async: 5-minute Loom walkthrough of tools and shared folder
- Require interview slots booked and confirmed before Day 0 gate passes
- Send "what to expect at readout" one-pager at kickoff: format, attendee expectations, decision memo
- Day 1 agenda email sent 24h before kickoff: who attends, what they need to bring, what AMC will do
