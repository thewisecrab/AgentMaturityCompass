# BILLING & COLLECTIONS PLAYBOOK (REV_CFO_FINANCE)

## 1) Objective
Protect cashflow by enforcing fast, clear, and contract-aligned billing while maintaining a professional client experience.

---

## 2) Standard billing terms

### Sprint projects
- Billing: **70% upfront on signature + 30% on delivery signoff**
- Work begins only after upfront receipt
- Final deliverables released after final payment confirmation (unless contract exception)

### Retainer projects
- Billing: **100% monthly advance**
- Invoice date: 25th of prior month (or day of signup for first month)
- Due date: 1st–5th of service month
- Grace period: 7 calendar days

### Add-ons / change requests
- < ₹75,000: 100% advance
- >= ₹75,000: 50% advance + 50% on completion

---

## 3) Invoice operations SOP

### Before issuing invoice (control checklist)
1. Signed MSA/SoW present
2. Correct legal entity + GST details confirmed
3. PO number (if client requires) captured
4. Scope milestone and amount match contract
5. Tax treatment validated

### Invoice issue cadence
- **Daily at 4 PM:** issue all triggered invoices same day
- **No batching beyond 24 hours** once milestone/trigger condition is met

### Required invoice fields
- Client legal name and billing address
- Tax identifiers
- Invoice number/date/due date
- Line-item description tied to SoW milestone
- Bank/payment details
- Late fee clause reference (if contract permits)

---

## 4) Collections ladder (dunning sequence)

### T-3 days before due
- Friendly reminder email with invoice and payment link/details

### Due date (D0)
- Reminder + confirmation request from AP contact

### D+3
- Follow-up email + WhatsApp/phone to AP owner

### D+7 (grace limit)
- Escalation to business stakeholder + account owner
- Service continuation reviewed for high-risk accounts

### D+15
- Formal overdue notice
- Pause new scope/add-ons until payment plan is signed

### D+30
- Leadership escalation; legal review for recovery route
- Client marked "credit hold"

## 5) Weekly cashflow checkpoints (mandatory Friday review)

## Weekly template
| Metric | Target | Week Actual | Status |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Opening cash | — |  |  |
| Total due this week | — |  |  |
| Cash collected this week | >=90% of due |  |  |
| Collection efficiency | >=90% |  |  |
| DSO | <=18 days |  |  |
| 31+ day receivables | <=8% of total AR |  |  |
| Next 14-day inflow/outflow coverage | >=1.3x |  |  |

### Checkpoint decisions
- If collection efficiency <85% for 2 consecutive weeks -> mandatory founder escalation on top 5 overdue invoices.
- If 31+ day AR >10% -> freeze non-essential delivery for chronic late-payers pending resolution.
- If DSO >21 days -> tighten terms for all new deals (more advance-heavy).

---

## 6) Roles and ownership (RACI-lite)
- **Finance (owner):** issue invoices, AR aging, weekly collections report
- **Account Manager:** relationship follow-ups, payment unblocker
- **Sales/AE:** commercial escalation where needed
- **Delivery Lead:** milestone proof documentation for billing defensibility
- **Founder/COO:** executive escalation for >D+15 high-value dues

---

## 7) Credit policy
- Default credit: none (advance-first model)
- Exception approval required for net terms >7 days
- Hard limits:
  - Max outstanding exposure/client: 1.25x monthly billing
  - Auto-credit hold if breached

---

## 8) Dispute handling protocol
1. Log dispute within 24h (reason, amount, owner)
2. Isolate disputed and undisputed amounts
3. Ask for undisputed payment immediately
4. Resolve evidence package within 3 business days
5. Close dispute with written confirmation

---

## 9) Reporting pack (weekly)
- AR aging summary (bucketed)
- Top 10 overdue invoices (amount + days + owner + action)
- Collections forecast for next 2 weeks
- Risk flag list (probability-weighted)
- Cash runway impact note
