# AMC Proof Stack

## 1
- **Claim:** Teams that move AI agents from pilots into production often see reliability gaps that were not visible in early demos.
- **Compliant framing:** "Teams that ship agents often discover behavior differences between demo environments and live workloads; reliability depends on runtime controls, not just model capability."
- **Evidence basis:** Industry incident patterns commonly show model/agent failures tied to orchestration, context drift, API/tool behavior changes, and untested edge cases when workload scale changes. This is a known production-readiness gap in software reliability practice.
- **Confidence tier:** MEDIUM
- **Use case:** LP, outreach, sales call, proposal

## 2
- **Claim:** Trust failures in enterprise AI programs usually create buying friction before feature quality does.
- **Compliant framing:** "Enterprise prospects often pause because they need confidence in escalation behavior, consistency, and risk handling before they can proceed, even when demos look promising."
- **Evidence basis:** Known failure mode across AI adoption initiatives: trust concerns (unexpected output, poor accountability, opaque failure handling) increase stall points in reviews and procurement approvals.
- **Confidence tier:** HIGH
- **Use case:** LP, outreach, sales call

## 3
- **Claim:** Unclear ownership and response procedures for AI mistakes increase the perceived cost of adoption in enterprise sales cycles.
- **Compliant framing:** "When buyers cannot map who owns incident response and exception handling, deal progress often slows and legal/risk teams raise more questions."
- **Evidence basis:** Governance and vendor-risk practices generally require clear ownership, logging, and remediation pathways. Lack of these controls is a standard reason for procurement friction in software procurement workflows.
- **Confidence tier:** HIGH
- **Use case:** outreach, proposal, sales call

## 4
- **Claim:** Mature governance evidence (traceability, policy controls, approvals) is increasingly part of enterprise AI qualification, not a post-contract add-on.
- **Compliant framing:** "Teams that present explicit governance artifacts early tend to reduce review churn versus teams that rely on broad claims."
- **Evidence basis:** Published governance frameworks and common enterprise controls programs emphasize traceability, auditability, and human oversight as preconditions for adopting higher-risk AI workflows.
- **Confidence tier:** HIGH
- **Use case:** proposal, sales call

## 5
- **Claim:** A maturity score gives teams a common basis to compare readiness across functions and prioritize fixes.
- **Compliant framing:** "Teams using a structured maturity view can discuss gaps in the same language across engineering, security, and business stakeholders."
- **Evidence basis:** Maturity-model scoring is a recognized approach in process governance and operations; it reduces ambiguity by turning subjective opinions into comparable, reviewable criteria.
- **Confidence tier:** HIGH
- **Use case:** LP, proposal, sales call

## 6
- **Claim:** In production AI programs, measurement quality of evidence is often more decisive than the count of completed “checks.”
- **Compliant framing:** "A higher-confidence artifact (logs, test results, incident history, and role evidence) carries more weight than a self-asserted checklist."
- **Evidence basis:** Logical inference from verification standards: evidence quality is the basis of auditability and reduces false positives in readiness assessments; weak evidence creates overestimated readiness claims.
- **Confidence tier:** HIGH
- **Use case:** LP, outreach, proposal

## 7
- **Claim:** Structured third-party assessments typically surface high-impact risks faster than ad hoc internal diagnostics.
- **Compliant framing:** "Teams that are under pilot pressure can usually identify top-priority production risks sooner with a fixed-scope assessment framework than with scattered internal triage."
- **Evidence basis:** Pattern from reliability and readiness work: standardized intake, evidence templates, and predefined scoring rubrics reduce iteration loops compared with manually organizing stakeholders and artifacts from scratch.
- **Confidence tier:** MEDIUM
- **Use case:** outreach, LP, sales call

## 8
- **Claim:** Speed of assessment is not just convenience; it can preserve option value before scaling decisions lock in.
- **Compliant framing:** "A faster baseline can help teams avoid scaling from assumptions before critical reliability and governance gaps are seen."
- **Evidence basis:** Project governance logic: early discovery of blocking risks lowers rework and prevents late-stage redesigns that are more expensive to unwind once production momentum has started.
- **Confidence tier:** MEDIUM
- **Use case:** LP, proposal

## 9
- **Claim:** Explicit confidence tags and scope assumptions reduce downstream disputes about what the score actually means.
- **Compliant framing:** "Teams are better protected when conclusions are labeled with confidence and explicit assumptions, instead of single-point claims presented as certainty."
- **Evidence basis:** Risk communication best practice in risk assessments: separating observed fact, inference, and assumption improves decision quality and reduces interpretation mismatch between stakeholders.
- **Confidence tier:** HIGH
- **Use case:** proposal, sales call

## 10
- **Claim:** In multi-agent programs, small reliability gaps can compound across workflows, so trust boundaries are central to risk communication.
- **Compliant framing:** "Teams coordinating multiple agents often benefit from explicit boundaries, scope constraints, and rollback controls so a single failure does not cascade."
- **Evidence basis:** Logical inference from agentic system design and control-plane risk patterns: increased autonomy and connectivity increase blast-radius unless permissioning, guardrails, and recovery controls are explicit.
- **Confidence tier:** MEDIUM
- **Use case:** LP, outreach, sales call
