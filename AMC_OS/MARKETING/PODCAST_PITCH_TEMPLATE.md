**The production readiness gap is becoming the defining bottleneck for enterprise AI in 2026.**

Most AI teams we engage with have shipped agents. Many of those agents are running in production. But when you ask "how do you know it's actually ready?" — reliable under load, trustworthy enough for operators to act on, designed with intentional oversight, traceable when something goes wrong — the answer is almost always: "we're not sure."

The tools for model evaluation are mature. The tools for agent maturity assessment are not. And that gap is where production incidents happen, where internal AI investments lose credibility, and where AI roadmaps stall.

We built a structured maturity framework — scored, evidence-backed, and calibrated to the specific deployment context — because we couldn't find one that existed. The Compass Sprint is our 5-day engagement that gives teams a baseline and a prioritized roadmap.

I think your audience would find value in a conversation covering:
- What "production maturity" actually means for AI agents (specific dimensions, not philosophies)
- The most common gaps we find — and why they're systematically missed
- What a team can do in 30 days to meaningfully close their maturity gap

I can share our framework document in advance, and I'm happy to structure the conversation however works for your format.

Would a 30-minute intro call make sense?

**Target shows:** No Priors, Lenny's Podcast, Acquired (long-horizon), Turpentine AI  
**Length:** ~400–450 words  
**Tone:** Narrative, honest, specific — tells the "why we built this" story  
**Primary hook:** Founder noticed a gap nobody else was talking about, built the thing that should exist

---

**Subject Line options (pick one):**
- `The question every AI team asks but can't answer — and what we built to fix it`
- `Guest pitch: How we discovered the AI production readiness gap (the hard way)`
- `[Show name] guest pitch — founder story: building the maturity infrastructure for AI agents`

---

**Body:**

Hi [Host First Name],

[Specific personal reference — e.g., "I heard your conversation with X about Y, and your framing of 'shipped vs. ready' stuck with me because it's exactly what we're solving."]

I'm [Your Name], founder of AMC. I wanted to share the story behind why we started this, because I think it's the kind of problem your audience will immediately recognize.

**The story:**

When I was [brief background context — e.g., "working with AI-first teams on delivery"], I kept running into the same conversation. A team would show me their agent. It was impressive. It did the thing. And then I'd ask: "How do you know it's ready for production?"

Every single time, the room got quiet.

Not because the team was naive — these were smart engineers and product leaders. But because nobody had ever given them a structured way to answer that question. "Production-ready AI agent" had no agreed definition. No scoring rubric. No baseline.

So they were making the call on intuition, on "it hasn't broken yet," on a demo that went well. And eventually, some of those agents would surface a gap — an oversight failure, an operator trust breakdown, a traceability problem — that everyone wished they'd found before it reached users.

We built AMC because that problem is solvable with structure. The Compass Sprint is a 5-day engagement: we score your AI maturity across four dimensions (reliability, operator trust, oversight design, evidence quality), surface the gaps that matter most for your specific context, and hand you a prioritized execution roadmap. Fixed scope. Fixed price. No vague consulting.

What I'd love to discuss on [Show Name]:
- The "quiet room" moment — why even sophisticated AI teams don't have a maturity baseline
- What we learned building the scoring rubric (the dimensions that surprised us)
- What the AI maturity market looks like in 2026, and what teams need to stop doing immediately
- The 30/60/90 path from "we shipped something" to "we know it's ready"

I think this would resonate with your audience because [specific audience-show connection — e.g., "your listeners are the exact decision-makers navigating this gap right now"].

Happy to jump on a 20-minute call to see if there's a fit.

[Your name]  
[Title], AMC  
[Email] | [LinkedIn URL]

**Customization notes:**
- The "quiet room" story is the core narrative moment — do NOT remove it; it's the most resonant hook
