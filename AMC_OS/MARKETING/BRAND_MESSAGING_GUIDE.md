## 3. VALUE PROPOSITION BY ICP SEGMENT

### Segment 1 — AI-First B2B Service Firms (10–200 employees)
**Their situation:** They're shipping AI agents as core product or delivery infrastructure. They move fast. They've probably shipped something that works — but "works" and "production-mature" are different things they haven't formally separated yet.

**Primary pain:** No formal maturity baseline → no shared language for "ready" → roadmap driven by whoever's loudest, not evidence.

**Value prop:**
> "A Compass Sprint gives your team the evidence-backed baseline you need to stop debating readiness and start acting on it — with a roadmap that your engineers, product team, and leadership can all agree on."

**Resonant proof framing:**
> "Teams at your stage commonly discover 3–5 high-priority maturity gaps that weren't on their roadmap before the Sprint — gaps in oversight design, trust infrastructure, or evidence quality that matter more than the features they were planning to ship."

---

### Segment 2 — Mid-Market SaaS Copilot / Agent Teams
**Their situation:** They have a core SaaS product and they're adding copilots, embedded agents, or AI automation. AI is a feature, not the whole product — which means it has to earn trust from existing users and meet the bar set by the rest of the product.

**Primary pain:** Unclear what "production-ready AI" means in the context of their existing product quality bar. Board-level pressure to show AI progress. Risk of shipping something that damages trust in the core product.

**Value prop:**
> "The Compass Sprint gives you a defensible, scored assessment of your AI maturity that you can take to your board, your users, and your team — and a sequenced roadmap that integrates with your existing product development cycle."

**Resonant proof framing:**
> "Mid-market SaaS teams adding copilots frequently underestimate the trust and oversight infrastructure required for enterprise users to adopt AI features — the Sprint surfaces exactly these gaps before they become adoption blockers."

---

### Segment 3 — Agencies Needing White-Label Maturity Methodology
**Their situation:** They're selling AI services — strategy, implementation, or ongoing support — and they need a credible, structured methodology that differentiates their work from the hundreds of generalist agencies now offering "AI services."

**Primary pain:** No proprietary framework → commoditized pitch → forced to compete on price. Clients can't tell the difference between rigorous AI delivery and AI-flavored consulting.

**Value prop:**
> "The Compass Sprint gives your agency a branded, evidence-backed maturity methodology you can white-label, cite in proposals, and use to demonstrate structured AI delivery rigor to sophisticated buyers."

**Resonant proof framing:**
> "Agencies that use a structured maturity framework in their proposals consistently differentiate from generalist competitors — buyers can see the difference between structured evidence-based delivery and generic AI consulting."

## 4. FORBIDDEN PHRASES (Never Use)

These sound like generic consulting, make unverifiable claims, or activate buyer skepticism. **Ban from all external copy.**

| Forbidden | Why banned |
|---|---|
| "Cutting-edge AI" | Meaningless; every AI vendor says this |
| "Transformative" | Vague; no specific outcome |
| "Leverage AI" | Jargon; says nothing about what we do |
| "AI-powered solutions" | Generic AI marketing phrase |
| "Unlock the power of AI" | Cliché; sounds like a webinar ad from 2021 |
| "Seamlessly" | Overused; signals generic copy |
| "Robust" | Empty adjective; be specific instead |
| "End-to-end" | Vague; what does end-to-end mean here? |
| "Best-in-class" | Superlative claim without proof |
| "Future-proof" | Unverifiable; nobody can promise this |
| "Holistic" | Consulting buzzword; says nothing |
| "Synergy" | Never. |
| "Guaranteed results" | Prohibited by claims policy |
| "Will increase revenue" | Prohibited by claims policy |
| "Risk-free" | Prohibited by claims policy |
| "AI-ready" (without definition) | Vague; define what ready means specifically |
| "Strategic partner" | Empty phrase; be specific about the relationship |
| "We help you succeed" | Generic; too soft, says nothing |
| "We work with leading companies" | Unverifiable superlative without specifics |
| "Our proprietary methodology" | Triggering to buyers unless you show the methodology |

## 5. PREFERRED VOCABULARY

Use these words and frames consistently across all copy:

### Core Vocabulary
| Preferred term | Meaning / context |
|---|---|
| **Maturity** | Where an AI agent actually stands on a scored, evidence-based scale |
| **Baseline** | The starting scored state before improvements begin |
| **Evidence-backed** | Findings supported by specific signals, not intuition |
| **Gap analysis** | Ranked list of what's missing or weak and why it matters |
| **Execution roadmap** | Prioritized, sequenced action plan — not a strategy doc |
| **Production-ready** | Meets the bar for real-world deployment; always defined specifically |
| **Operator trust** | The degree to which human operators rely on and act on agent outputs |
| **Escalation path** | Defined process for when an agent fails or is uncertain |
| **Quality gate** | Explicit checkpoint before an agent action executes |
| **Human oversight** | Design element that keeps humans informed and in control |
| **Scored** | Assessed against a defined rubric with numeric or tiered output |
| **Calibrated** | Adjusted to your specific context, not applied generically |
| **Structured** | Follows a defined process; not improvised |
| **Prioritized by impact** | Sequenced by expected business/risk outcome, not by ease |
| **Directional** | Observed pattern or evidence that points in a direction; not a guarantee |
| **Decision memo** | Concise written output that enables a clear go/no-go call |
| **Evidence quality** | How well AI agent outputs can be explained, traced, and verified |

### Tone Descriptors
- **Specific > Vague:** Always name the dimension, the gap, the action. Never describe outcomes generally.
- **Honest > Aspirational:** Acknowledge what the Sprint doesn't do. It builds trust.
- **Confident > Tentative:** State findings clearly. Use "we observe," "we assess," "we recommend" — not "we think maybe."
- **Operational > Visionary:** Buyers of a Compass Sprint are not buying a vision. They're buying a plan they can execute next Monday.

## 6. MESSAGING DO / DON'T QUICK REFERENCE

| Situation | ✅ Say this | ❌ Not this |
|---|---|---|
| Describing the Sprint | "A 5-day, fixed-scope engagement that scores your AI maturity and delivers a prioritized roadmap" | "A comprehensive AI transformation program" |
| Describing outcomes | "You'll leave with a scored scorecard, gap analysis, and execution roadmap" | "We'll help you succeed with AI" |
| Describing proof | "Teams at this stage commonly discover oversight gaps they weren't tracking" | "Our clients see dramatic results" |
| Handling risk | "Outcomes depend on team readiness and implementation; results vary" | "It works, period." |
| Describing our approach | "Evidence-backed, scored, prioritized — specific to your context" | "We take a holistic, strategic approach" |
| On price | "$5,000 fixed. No surprises. Clear scope." | "Investment varies based on your needs" |
| On commitment | "No implementation lock-in. Sprint stands alone." | "The beginning of a long-term partnership" |

## 7. CHANNEL-SPECIFIC TONE ADJUSTMENTS

| Channel | Tone tweak |
|---|---|
| **Landing page** | Direct, specific, slightly formal. No punchlines. Trust-building first. |
| **LinkedIn posts** | More conversational, observation-led, first-person patterns we've noticed. |
| **Cold email / outreach** | Ultra-concise, problem-first, single CTA. No flourishes. |
| **Sales call** | Conversational but precise. Match buyer's language; use their words back. |
| **Proposals / SOWs** | Formal but not stiff. Concrete scope language, zero vagueness. |
| **Executive readout** | Evidence-led, decision-ready framing. No selling — just findings and recommendations. |
