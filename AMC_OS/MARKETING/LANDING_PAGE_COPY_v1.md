## ─── SECTION 2: PROBLEM BLOCK ───

### Heading
> **"If your AI roadmap feels like organized guessing, it probably is."**

---

### Pain Point 1 — Building Without a Baseline
**"You shipped. But 'shipped' isn't the same as 'ready.'"**

Your team pushed the agent to production. It's running. But nobody's formally assessed whether the quality gates are real, whether the escalation logic is sound, or whether operators actually trust what it's doing. One failure at the wrong moment doesn't just break a workflow — it breaks credibility with leadership and customers at the same time.

Teams that don't establish a maturity baseline before scaling often discover their biggest gaps when the cost of finding them is highest.

---

### Pain Point 2 — No Shared Language for "AI Readiness"
**"Your engineers say it's fine. Your leadership wants proof. Your customers just want it to work."**

Without a common maturity framework, everyone in the room means something different when they say "ready." Engineers are focused on error rates. Leadership wants ROI signals. Product wants user adoption. The result: every planning meeting revisits the same unresolved questions, and prioritization defaults to whoever made the loudest argument last week.

---

### Pain Point 3 — Roadmaps Driven by Noise, Not Evidence
**"When everything is a priority, nothing is."**

Without evidence-backed scoring, your sprint planning reflects urgency — not importance. Critical gaps in human oversight design, trust infrastructure, and failure-mode handling stay invisible because nobody has a framework to surface them. The real risks compound quietly while the visible fires get all the sprints.

---

### Bridge Line
> The Compass Sprint closes this gap. You walk out with a scored baseline, a ranked list of what actually matters, and a roadmap your team can execute on immediately — not after another quarter of internal debates.

## ─── SECTION 8: FAQ ───

### Heading
> **"Common questions — answered straight."**

---

**Q1: How long does the Sprint actually take — and what does my team need to commit?**

The engagement runs 5 business days from kickoff. Your team's time commitment is lighter than you'd expect: two to four stakeholder interviews (45–60 min each, schedulable async), plus the 60-minute Executive Readout at the end. We handle all synthesis, scoring, and roadmap construction in between. If your team can make time for the interviews and the readout, you have everything you need.

---

**Q2: What information do we need to provide?**

Three inputs make the sprint most useful:
1. **Stakeholder interviews** (2–4 people, ideally covering engineering, product, and operations/leadership)
2. **Current workflow and tooling context** — how your agents are deployed, monitored, and handled when things go wrong
3. **A baseline KPI snapshot** — even rough metrics on what you're tracking today

You don't need polished documentation. We work with what exists and surface what's missing as part of the gap analysis.

---

**Q3: Is this strategy work or implementation work?**

Strategy only. The Compass Sprint produces a scored assessment, a gap analysis, and a prioritized roadmap. We do not write code, build workflows, configure tools, or implement anything during the Sprint. If you need implementation support afterward, we can discuss whether AMC is the right fit — but that's a separate conversation with separate scope and pricing.

---

**Q4: We're a small team (under 20 people). Is this right for us?**

Yes — if you're already building or deploying AI agents. Small teams often benefit most from a Compass Sprint because they have the least redundancy to absorb maturity gaps, and the roadmap gives them a clear prioritization rationale when capacity is limited. The engagement scales to team size; the output is calibrated to your context, not built for enterprise complexity.

---

**Q5: What if we're not satisfied with the output?**

We'll tell you clearly at the scoping call if we don't think the Sprint is the right fit for where you are — and we'll decline to take the engagement if we can't deliver something useful. If we proceed and the output doesn't meet the scope commitments, we'll work to resolve it. What we won't do is guarantee specific business outcomes. The Sprint delivers a scored assessment and a roadmap; what your team does with it determines the downstream impact.
