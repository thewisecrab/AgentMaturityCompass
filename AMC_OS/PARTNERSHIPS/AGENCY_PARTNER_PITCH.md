## 1. PROGRAM OVERVIEW

**The AMC Agency Partner Program** enables AI-forward agencies to offer a branded, evidence-backed AI maturity assessment to their clients — without building the methodology themselves.

Agencies white-label the **Compass Sprint** under their own brand, deliver it (or co-deliver with AMC), and earn a structured revenue share. AMC handles the rubric, scoring framework, reporting templates, and QA backstop. Agencies keep the client relationship.

### Who this is for (ideal agency partner profile)
- Digital agencies already selling AI strategy, implementation, or optimization services
- Boutique AI consultancies who need a structured maturity framework to differentiate proposals
- Growth / RevOps agencies who are fielding client requests for AI governance or "AI readiness assessments"
- Any agency where the pitch "we'll assess your AI maturity" would upgrade or expand existing client relationships

## 2. VALUE PROPOSITION FOR AGENCIES

### The core problem agencies face
Every AI agency now claims to do AI implementation. Without a proprietary assessment methodology, agencies are forced to compete on price, portfolio, or brand — all of which erode margins and lengthen sales cycles.

> Buyers are asking: *"How do we know if our AI is actually ready?"*  
> Most agencies don't have a structured answer.

### What the AMC partnership solves

| Without AMC | With AMC White-Label |
|---|---|
| Generic AI audit offered as a vague deliverable | Scored, rubric-based Compass Sprint with defined outputs |
| No clear differentiation from 100 other agencies | Proprietary-looking assessment methodology with scored output |
| Manual maturity analysis with inconsistent quality | Structured framework with QA backstop from AMC |
| Pricing pressure from generalist competitors | Premium-positioned, methodology-backed engagement |
| Limited expansion revenue in existing accounts | New upsell motion: "Would you like us to run a maturity assessment first?" |

### Revenue upside for agencies (illustrative assumptions — not guarantees)
- A $5,000 Compass Sprint billed to a client = $1,500–$2,000 revenue share retained by agency (30–40% model)
- Agencies with 10 active retainer clients: 2–4 maturity assessments per quarter = $3,000–$8,000 additional quarterly revenue
- Sprint-to-retainer conversion creates follow-on scopes the agency can also hold or co-deliver

> **Assumption:** Revenue share projections are illustrative. Actual outcomes depend on agency client mix, sales cycle, and conversion rates.

## 4. REVENUE SHARE MODEL

### Tier Structure (v1 — open to negotiation for founding partners)

| Tier | Entry criteria | Agency revenue share | AMC backstop support | Co-marketing |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Registered Partner** | Signed agreement | 20% of Sprint fee | None (self-deliver) | Logo listing |
| **Certified Partner** | 1 completed delivery + QA review | 30% of Sprint fee | QA review per delivery | Blog feature + LinkedIn post |
| **Strategic Partner** | 3+ deliveries + quarterly volume | 40% of Sprint fee + 10% of retainer conversion | Priority QA backstop + co-delivery | Joint webinar + case study |

### Revenue Share Mechanics
- Sprint billed by **agency to client at agency's discretion** (agency controls pricing)
- Agency invoices AMC for the revenue share amount **or** AMC licenses rubric at fixed monthly fee
- **Option A (Commission):** Agency self-delivers, retains full client fee, pays AMC a 10–20% licensing/QA fee per Sprint
- **Option B (Rev Share):** AMC co-delivers, agency takes 30–40% of total Sprint fee
- **Retainer tail:** If client converts to an AMC retainer, agency receives 10% of retainer MRR for first 6 months

> **Assumption:** Pricing structures are subject to negotiation. Option A / Option B terms above are starting positions.

### Founding Partner Terms (first 5 agencies)
- 6-month exclusivity window in their niche/vertical (where feasible)
- Highest rev share tier from day 1
- Direct access to AMC founder for QA questions
- Co-design input on rubric v2

## 7. PITCH OBJECTION RESPONSES

| "We already do audits ourselves" | "What does your scoring rubric look like? We find most agencies have strong domain instincts but no consistent scoring anchor — which makes QA and case study development harder." |
