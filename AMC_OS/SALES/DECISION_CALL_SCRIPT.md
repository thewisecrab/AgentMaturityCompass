# DECISION CALL SCRIPT — AMC AE Closer
> Version: 1.0 | Owner: REV_ACCOUNT_EXEC_CLOSER | Lever: B — Conversion  
> Use for any PACT-DO 8–12 opportunity. Duration target: 30–45 minutes.  
> Fill [BRACKETS] before the call. Read the script conversationally — not verbatim.

---

## PRE-CALL CHECKLIST (5 min before dialing)
- [ ] PACT-DO score documented and ≥ 8
- [ ] All 5 exit criteria confirmed or gaps identified
- [ ] Proposal/SOW ready to send within 2 hours post-call
- [ ] Objections anticipated from CRM notes — responses prepped
- [ ] Champion prepped: did they confirm DM will attend?
- [ ] Kickoff date selected and delivery team notified to hold slot

---

## OPENING (2 min)

> *Goal: Set the agenda, signal efficiency, earn trust.*

**AE:** "Thanks for making time, [NAME]. I want to make sure this call is worth every minute — so here's what I'd like to do: I'll do a quick recap of what I heard last time, you tell me if I got it right or correct it, and then we'll either align on a clear path forward today, or identify exactly what's in the way. Sound good?"

*[Pause. Let them agree.]*

"Perfect. Here's what I understood from our last conversation:

You're dealing with **[pain in their words]**. The impact you described was **[quantified cost/risk]**. And the reason this has to move now is **[their urgency driver — board date, launch date, exec pressure]**. Is that still accurate?"

*[Listen. Adjust if anything has changed. If situation has changed significantly, re-score PACT-DO.]*

---

## DISCOVERY PROBES — PACT-DO ALIGNED (10 min)

> *Use only the probes you need. Skip dimensions already scored 2. Probe hard on any dimension scored 0 or 1.*

### P — Problem Severity (if score < 2)
- "Since we last spoke, has anything changed about how visible this problem is to leadership?"
- "If you had to put a number on the monthly cost of the current situation — revenue, rework, or risk — what would you say?"
- "What happens operationally and commercially if nothing changes in the next 30 days?"

### A — Authority Access (if score < 2)
- "I want to make sure we have the right people involved for a decision. Who will ultimately sign, and who else needs to be confident before that happens?"
- "Is [DM NAME] joining today, or will you be the one walking them through the recommendation?"
- "If there are concerns from [DM/PROCUREMENT], what are they most likely to raise?"

### C — Commitment Timeline (if score < 2)
- "Help me understand the real decision timeline. Is [DATE] still the target, or has something shifted internally?"
- "What specific event or deadline is driving that date?"
- "If we can't start by [DATE], what's the downstream impact on [their initiative/KPI]?"

### T — Technical Readiness (if score < 2)
- "Who is the internal owner for implementation — the person who would actually manage the integration work on your side?"
- "Walk me through the stack we'd need to touch in week 1. Any red flags or access issues?"
- "Is IT/Eng in the loop, or would this proposal be the first they hear about it?"

### D — Data Availability (if score < 2)
- "What does your current baseline look like for [target metric]? Do you have that number handy?"
- "Can your team pull that data before kickoff, or is that itself a blocker we need to plan around?"
- "How long does it typically take your data team to pull a 90-day snapshot?"

### O — Outcome Urgency (if score < 2)
- "What metric must move in the first 30 days for this to be declared a win internally?"
- "Is that target tied to anyone's formal OKR or performance goal — or is it more of a team aspiration?"
- "Who would you show the results to, and what would they need to see to call this a success?"

---

## QUALIFICATION TRANSITION (1 min)

> *Signal your read on fit. Create mutual investment in the path forward.*

**If strong fit (PACT-DO 10–12):**

"Based on everything you've shared, I think this is a strong fit. The problem is real, you have a clear timeline, and the outcome is well-defined. I want to propose a specific path forward and see if we can lock the start date before we hang up. Can I do that?"

**If moderate fit (PACT-DO 8–9):**

"I think there's a solid case here. Before I present a formal recommendation, I want to make sure [specific gap] is resolved — because that's the one thing that could slow us down after you approve. Can we address that now?"

**If gaps remain (PACT-DO 6–7):**

"Here's where I am: I see real potential, but I'd be doing you a disservice if I sent a proposal before [specific gap: e.g., 'we confirm your VP Eng is aligned on integration']. Let's set a specific action: [action + owner + date]. Once that's done, I can turn a proposal around in 24 hours."

---

## OFFER PRESENTATION (5 min)

> *Present one recommendation. Don't make them choose between three options mid-call.*

**AE:** "Given your goal to **[target outcome]** by **[date]**, here's what I recommend:

**The AMC Compass Sprint** — 5 business days, $5,000 fixed fee.

Here's what you walk away with:
- A scored assessment of your current agent/AI setup against AMC benchmarks
- The top 3–5 gaps, ranked by impact — so your team knows exactly what to fix first
- A 30/60/90 roadmap your leadership can approve and execute on
- A 1-page executive memo that makes the internal approval case for next phase

We need 2–4 stakeholder interviews, your current workflow docs, and a KPI baseline — all coordinated in Day 1 so we don't slow you down.

We kick off within 48 hours of you approving today. I have **[KICKOFF DATE]** held for you.

Does this align with what you need?"

*[Pause. Let them respond. Do NOT fill the silence.]*

---

## TRIAL CLOSE LANGUAGE (use throughout)

> *Layer these in naturally — not all at once.*

- "On a scale of 1–10, how well does this match what you're trying to solve?"  
  → *If < 8:* "What would make it a 9 or 10?"  
  → *If they name something:* "Can we resolve that now?"

- "Is there anyone not on this call whose concerns we should address before you approve?"

- "If this proposal addresses everything you just raised, are you prepared to approve by **[decision date]**?"

- "Do you want to optimize for fastest result — or lowest initial risk? Because I can adjust the starting scope based on that."

- "If we hit the first milestone by **[Day 5 date]**, would that validate the investment internally?"

---

## OBJECTION RESPONSES

> *Listen fully. Acknowledge. Reframe. Don't cave.*

### 💬 "The price is too high."

"I hear you. Let me reframe for a second — you said this problem is costing **[~$X/month]**. The Sprint is $5,000 fixed and delivers a clear, actionable plan in 5 days. Most clients recoup that in the first workflow improvement we identify.

That said, if approval is the issue, I can restructure the payment: **$2,500 today, $2,500 on delivery**. The scope and outcome stay the same. Would that make the approval easier?"

*[If they push further → move to scope narrowing, not discount. See concession ladder.]*

---

### 💬 "The timing isn't right."

"Understood. Help me understand — is it timing because of internal capacity, budget cycle, or something else?

[Listen.]

Here's what I've seen work: the teams most pressed for time are often the ones who most benefit from a structured 5-day sprint, because it removes the decision bottleneck rather than adding to it. We run the coordination; you just show up for 4 interview slots.

What would need to change internally for timing to work in the next two weeks?"

---

### 💬 "I need to think about it." / "I need more time."

"Of course — I want you to feel good about this. Can I ask: what's the specific thing you need to think through? Because if it's about scope, commercials, or timeline, we can address that right now. If it's something else, I want to make sure I'm not leaving a real concern unresolved."

*[Listen. Address the specific concern.]*

"So the one thing standing between you and a clear yes is **[their specific concern]**. If I can resolve that — either now or with a follow-up by **[date]** — would you be ready to approve?"

---

### 💬 "I need to get approval from [boss / board / procurement]."

"Completely reasonable. Let me make that approval as easy as possible for you.

Two things that help: First, can I send you an 8-line summary right now — problem, outcome, scope, fee, timeline, risk controls, decision date — formatted so you can paste it directly into an email to **[DM NAME]**?

Second, would it be useful for me to join a 20-minute call with **[DM NAME]** to handle any questions directly? That usually shortens the approval process from a week to a day.

Which of those would move things faster on your side?"

---

### 💬 "We're looking at other options."

"That's smart — this is a real investment and you should compare. Can I ask: what are the 2–3 criteria you're using to decide?

[Listen.]

Here's what I'd ask you to compare on: specific deliverables (not just 'an assessment'), fixed vs. open-ended price, and time to decision-ready output. Most alternatives are either broader (longer runway, higher cost) or shallower (a report with no roadmap).

I'll map our proposal directly to your criteria so you can make a clean comparison by **[decision date]**. What's the date you want to finalize this?"

---

## CONCESSION LADDER
> Follow in order. Do NOT skip ahead. Do NOT offer concessions preemptively.

**Step 1 — Payment Terms**  
*Trade: restructured payment schedule. Keep total fee at $5,000.*  
"I can split this 50% now / 50% on delivery. Total fee stays the same — does that make approval easier?"

**Step 2 — Scope Narrowing**  
*Trade: reduce deliverables to 1–2 highest-ROI outputs. Price adjusts proportionally only if scope drops significantly.*  
"If the priority is just the gap analysis and roadmap — not the full maturity scorecard — we can tighten scope and reduce the fee to **[$3,500]** for that focused output. Does that fit better?"

**Step 3 — Path Downgrade**  
*Trade: move from Sprint to a smaller diagnostic engagement. Hold pipeline open for future Sprint/Pilot.*  
"If $5k isn't the right starting point, we can run a focused 90-minute AI Readiness Session for **[$1,500]** — you get a prioritized gap list and a clear brief on whether a Sprint makes sense. Many clients use that to build the internal case. Want to start there?"

**Step 4 — Time-Bound Concession**  
*Trade: a meaningful but non-destructive add-on (extra stakeholder interview, faster delivery) in exchange for signature by a specific date.*  
"If you can approve by **[DATE — 48h from now]**, I'll include a second executive readout session at no extra charge — so you can share findings with two stakeholder groups instead of one. After that date, I can't hold the kickoff slot or this pricing. Does that work?"

> **STOP HERE.** If all four steps are exhausted and prospect still won't commit, move to close ask — not further concessions.

---

## CLOSE ASK (1 min)

> *State it cleanly. No hedging. No apologizing for asking.*

**AE:** "I want to be direct with you, [NAME] — and I think you appreciate that.

Everything we've discussed tells me this is the right move at the right time for **[COMPANY]**. I have the kickoff slot held for **[DATE]**, the proposal is ready to send, and the SOW can be in your hands within two hours of your approval.

Here's my ask: can we agree today, and can I send you the final paperwork right now?"

*[Pause. Do NOT speak until they do.]*

---

**If YES:**

"Excellent. Sending the proposal and SOW now — [NAME], you'll see it in your inbox in the next few minutes. Let me know when you've signed and I'll send the onboarding checklist and kickoff confirmation immediately."

---

**If CONDITIONAL YES (pending approval/review):**

"Understood. Let's build a short mutual action plan so this doesn't drift. Here's what I'll do: send the 8-line internal summary + proposal within the hour. Here's what I need from you: get it to **[DM NAME]** today and confirm by **[DATE]**. I'll follow up at **[TIME]** if I haven't heard. Does that work?"

---

**If NO / NOT NOW:**

"I appreciate your honesty. Let me be equally direct: if the timing or fit isn't right, I'd rather close this cleanly and revisit when conditions change than drag it out.

Is this a 'not now because of [specific reason]' — in which case, when would be the right time? Or is it a 'not a fit' — in which case, I'll stop following up and wish you well?

Which is it?"

*[Log outcome in CRM. Set recycle date if "not now." DQ if "not a fit."]*
