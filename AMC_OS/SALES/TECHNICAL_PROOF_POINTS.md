# AMC Technical Proof Points (CTO / VP Engineering / AI Lead)

**Purpose:** Give sales to answer deep technical credibility questions during demos and calls.

Each proof point format: **Claim → Evidence basis → How to demonstrate → Relevant ICP**.

---

## 1) Claim: Scoring is repeatable and rubric-based, not ad hoc
- **Evidence basis:** Standardized scoring domains and weights documented in the assessment model; consistent output mapping from evidence inputs.
- **How to demonstrate:** Show two similar controls scored using same rubric with different artifact sets; explain why scores differ based on evidence confidence.
- **Relevant ICP:** CTO, VP of Engineering, AI Lead.

## 2) Claim: Every score has evidence linkage
- **Evidence basis:** Evidence vault requires artifact metadata, status tags (missing/weak/verified), and source references tied to each control.
- **How to demonstrate:** Open one control and trace it to supporting logs, policy docs, test traces, or governance notes.
- **Relevant ICP:** CTO, VP of Engineering, Security-oriented AI lead.

## 3) Claim: Confidence is explicit (coverage, freshness, verification)
- **Evidence basis:** Confidence decomposition surfaced in reporting fields (not hidden behind narrative). Confidence drops with stale, sparse, or unverified evidence.
- **How to demonstrate:** Show one control with high score but low confidence due to missing verification, and discuss decision implications.
- **Relevant ICP:** VP of Engineering, AI Program Manager, QA/Platform lead.

## 4) Claim: Prioritization is cost-aware and impact-aware
- **Evidence basis:** Gap ranking combines impact × effort × risk dimensions and is generated as a sortable list for planning.
- **How to demonstrate:** Use a sample gap list and reorder by impact/effort sliders; show executive-ready ranking changes.
- **Relevant ICP:** CTO, VP of Engineering, RevOps/PMO partner.

## 5) Claim: Output is governance-ready, not only operationally descriptive
- **Evidence basis:** Deliverables include decision memo and execution roadmap with owners, dependencies, acceptance criteria, and milestone milestones.
- **How to demonstrate:** Show a one-page decision memo export with sections mapped to leadership review needs.
- **Relevant ICP:** CTO, CPO, VP Operations, AI Lead.

## 6) Claim: Tooling stack agnostic and integration-light onboarding
- **Evidence basis:** Assessment takes documented workflows, artifacts, and existing observability inputs; no hard dependency on a single vendor ecosystem.
- **How to demonstrate:** Walk through ingestion of an existing spreadsheet/dashboard/API snapshot as source evidence.
- **Relevant ICP:** VP Engineering (heterogeneous stack), AI lead in mid-size teams.

## 7) Claim: Enables auditability and decision traceability
- **Evidence basis:** Versioned score changes and review checkpoints; auditable changes to criteria/outcome mapping.
- **How to demonstrate:** Show change history for a sample control and explain who updated/approved it.
- **Relevant ICP:** CTO, VP Engineering, CISO, Compliance-facing AI lead.

## 8) Claim: Supports both one-time assessment and recurring maturity cadence
- **Evidence basis:** Same framework used for sprint mode and retainer mode; recurring runs support drift comparison and roadmap refresh.
- **How to demonstrate:** Compare Day-1 baseline vs re-run snapshot and discuss delta narrative.
- **Relevant ICP:** CTO, AI Lead, Engineering manager with scaling team.

## 9) Claim: Security/compliance concern handling is embedded, not a bolt-on
- **Evidence basis:** Governance and reliability domains are included by default with evidence requirements and controls mapping.
- **How to demonstrate:** Display governance/security domain controls and show where evidence is missing/weak.
- **Relevant ICP:** CTO, CISO-linked AI initiatives, regulated sectors.

## 10) Claim: Deliverables reduce delivery risk through ownership clarity
- **Evidence basis:** Each recommendation includes named owners, dependencies, success criteria, and milestone date in 30/60/90 format.
- **How to demonstrate:** Show roadmap view and filter by owner/dependency to reveal accountability structure.
- **Relevant ICP:** CTO, VP Engineering, Implementation lead, Head of Product Engineering.

---

## 11) Claim: Practical for leadership communication
- **Evidence basis:** Executive readout and decision memo are standardized output artifacts; no need for ad hoc narrative preparation.
- **How to demonstrate:** Show executive memo generation and walk through one page in 90 seconds.
- **Relevant ICP:** CTO, VP Engineering, CIO-level sponsors.

## 12) Claim: Prevents hidden technical debt in AI operations
- **Evidence basis:** Recommends corrective tasks in ranked sequence tied to business impact and system-level dependencies.
- **How to demonstrate:** Select two adjacent roadmap items and tie them to measurable outcomes (reduced incident load, faster issue turn-around).
- **Relevant ICP:** VP Engineering, AI Lead, Site Reliability/Platform lead.

---

## Demo usage notes for SDR/AE
- Lead with proof points by role profile:
  - CTO: repeatability + auditable evidence.
  - VP Engineering: cadence + ownership + risk reduction.
  - AI Lead: roadmap quality + score confidence + implementation practicality.

- When challenged on credibility, move to specific artifact demonstration in the app before verbal claims.
