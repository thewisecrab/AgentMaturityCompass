# SALES SCRIPTS — IMMEDIATE CLOSE MOTION

## 1) Discovery Opener (2–3 min)
"Thanks for making time. I want to do this efficiently: first, where your agent systems are live and where trust breaks first; second, whether there’s a strong business case to act now; third, if there is, we’ll align on a practical path to launch. Sound good?"

## 2) Problem Deepening
"Walk me through the current workflow from trigger to outcome. Where does quality, speed, or trust fail most often?"

"What is that costing in missed revenue, cycle time, rework, or risk exposure?"

## 3) Authority + Process
"Who will sign this, and who else must be confident before approval?"

"If we agree this is a priority, what is your actual decision path and target approval date?"

## 4) Urgency + Outcome
"What metric must improve in the first 30 days for this to be a win?"

"If nothing changes this month, what happens operationally and commercially?"

## 5) Technical/Delivery Readiness
"Which systems must be integrated in week 1?"

"Who is your internal owner for implementation and adoption?"

---

## 6) Qualification Transition
"Based on what you shared, this looks like a [high/moderate] priority with [clear/unclear] decision path. If we can agree scope and commercials today, are you prepared to move to a start date this week?"

If yes:
"Great — I’ll recommend the most direct option and we’ll map a decision plan before we end this call."

If no:
"Understood. Then the right next step is to close the gaps: [DM access / success metric / owner]. I’ll send a short action list and we’ll reconvene after those are in place."

---

## 7) Offer Presentation Script (Close Call)
"Given your goal to **[target outcome]** by **[date]**, here’s the best-fit path:
- Scope: **[scope]**
- Time to value: **[timeline]**
- Deliverables: **[deliverables]**
- Investment: **[price]**
- Success criteria: **[measurable criteria]**

If this aligns, we can finalize now and lock kickoff for **[date]**."

## 8) Objection Handling (Concise)

### Budget
"Makes sense. Relative to the cost of delay you quantified (~[X]/month), this is designed to create operational clarity quickly. If we phase scope, would [Pilot/Sprint] make approval easier while preserving momentum?"

### Timing
"Understood. Teams usually feel timing pressure most when this is urgent. We can reduce lift by handling setup with your owner in one focused kickoff and weekly checkpoints. Would that remove the timing blocker?"

### Need to think / compare
"Absolutely. To keep this objective, what 2–3 criteria will decide? I can map our proposal directly to those so you can make a clean decision by [date]."

### No internal resources
"Then we should narrow scope to a low-friction launch. We’ll define exactly what we own vs. what your team approves so execution is realistic."

---

## 9) Trial Close Prompts
- "On a 1–10, how aligned is this with what you need?"
- "What would need to be true to make this a 10?"
- "If we resolve that now, are you ready to proceed?"

## 10) Hard Close (ethical, no pressure)
"I want to be respectful of your process and our capacity. If this is the right fit, let’s confirm today and reserve kickoff on **[date]**. If not, we should pause and revisit when conditions are right. Which direction is best?"

## 11) Post-Call Confirmation (email/slack template)
Subject: Decision Plan — [Company] x AMC

- Target outcome: [metric + deadline]
- Agreed scope: [summary]
- Commercials: [price + terms]
- Open items: [none / list]
- Decision date: [date/time]
- Proposed kickoff: [date/time]

"Reply ‘approved’ and we’ll issue final paperwork and onboarding checklist immediately."
