# OUTREACH SEQUENCES — REV_COPYWRITER_DIRECT_RESPONSE

## Purpose
Conversion-focused outbound sequences mapped to ICP segments with compliance-safe language and clear CTA flows.

**Primary lever:** LEVER A (Pipeline) with secondary LEVER B (Conversion)
**KPI target:** Positive reply rate, booked-call rate, and call-to-opportunity progression

---

## Compliance Guardrails (applies to every touch)
- Use qualified language: “designed to,” “can help,” “supports.”
- No guarantees or deterministic outcomes.
- Keep claims process-based unless verified data is cited.
- CTA asks for a short conversation, never implies guaranteed business results.
- Optional footer for email touches: “Results are not guaranteed and depend on team context, baseline, and execution.”

---

## CTA Flow Library (use consistently)
- **Low-friction CTA (Touch 1–2):** “Open to a 15-minute fit check next week?”
- **Value CTA (Touch 3–4):** “Want me to send a one-page teardown for your current workflow first?”
- **Decision CTA (Touch 5+):** “If priority/timing fit, we can map a Sprint/Pilot start window.”
- **Exit CTA (final touch):** “If now isn’t a priority, I can close the loop and follow up next quarter.”

## VARIANT A — STARTUP (10–50 employees, AI-first B2B service firms)
### Positioning angle
Speed-to-traction, lean execution, and reducing founder/operator load without overbuilding.

### Sequence structure
- 6 touches over 14 days
- Channels: Email + LinkedIn

### Touch 1 — Email (Problem + relevance)
**Subject:** Quick idea for {{Company}}’s {{workflow}}

Hi {{FirstName}},

Noticed {{observable context: hiring push / product launch / GTM change}} at {{Company}}.

Teams at your stage often lose momentum when {{workflow}} depends on ad hoc handoffs.
We help operators turn one high-friction workflow into a tighter weekly system with clear ownership and QA checkpoints.

If helpful, I can share a 1-page teardown of where cycle time typically slips (based on public signals + your use case).

Open to a 15-minute fit check next week?

— {{Sender}}

### Touch 2 — LinkedIn connect note
{{FirstName}} — enjoyed your take on {{topic}}. I work with AI-first teams to simplify one priority workflow at a time (without adding tool sprawl). Happy to share a short teardown if useful.

### Touch 3 — Email (Value give)
**Subject:** 3 friction points I’d test first at {{Company}}

Hi {{FirstName}},

Drafted a quick hypothesis list for {{Company}}’s {{workflow}}:
1) Intake criteria likely inconsistent across owners
2) QA/review gates probably happen too late
3) Escalation path may be unclear when edge cases appear

If any of these are close, we can map a practical 2-week fix path.
Would you like the one-page teardown before deciding on a call?

### Touch 4 — LinkedIn DM (social + clarity)
Sharing a lightweight benchmark: most teams improve speed first by tightening handoffs before adding more automation. If helpful, I can send a “what to fix in week 1” checklist.

### Touch 5 — Email (Offer bridge)
**Subject:** If this is priority, here’s the fastest path

If {{workflow}} is a Q{{quarter}} priority, the lowest-friction way to start is usually:
- **Compass Sprint (5 days):** diagnosis + 30/60/90 plan
or
- **Rapid Pilot (14 days):** one workflow scoped and shipped

No pressure—if timing is off, I can follow up later.
If timing is right, open to 15 minutes to confirm fit?

### Touch 6 — Email break-up (respectful close)
**Subject:** Should I close the loop?

Totally fine if this isn’t active right now.
Want me to close the loop for now, or check back next quarter?
