# Sprint-to-Retainer Expansion Playbook

**Objective:** Turn successful Compass Sprint outcomes into high-retention, high-value Continuous Maturity retainer adoption.

**Primary lever:** B — Conversion (phase transition) and C — Delivery-readiness (operationalization cadence)

---

## 1) Expansion premise
Sprint is discovery + prioritization; Retainer is operating discipline.

- Sprint creates the **baseline**, **ownership map**, and **roadmap**.
- Expansion is justified when the team demonstrates:
  1) repeated issue recurrence, 2) need for governance cadence, 3) measurable drift in quality/cost/compliance outcomes.

Think of Sprint as “diagnostic decision support” and Retainer as “operating system for sustained control.”

---

## 2) Timing triggers for expansion conversation

### Trigger A — 14–21 days after sprint completion
**Condition:** Client has consumed roadmap and identified at least 1 high-impact remediation.
**Talk track:**
"You now have a ranked plan; the next step is making this a repeatable loop so gains don’t decay."

### Trigger B — 30-day roadmap checkpoint
**Condition:** At least one top gap was addressed but confidence remains fragile.
**Talk track:**
"Implementation is working, and we can harden reliability by adding monthly rescoring and drift detection."

### Trigger C — Leadership asks for update cadence
**Condition:** Sponsor requests status format for review, governance, or board update.
**Talk track:**
"Retainer gives you recurring decision-grade reporting and prevents one-off, reactionary fixes."

### Trigger D — Expansion signs in operations
**Condition:** 3+ unplanned escalations across agents, repeated data-quality issues, or repeated approval churn.
**Talk track:**
"Your team is already generating enough data points that a retainer becomes more efficient than repeat ad hoc audits."

### Trigger E — Procurement readiness
**Condition:** Legal/security/compliance already engaged positively and asks for recurring scope.
**Talk track:**
"Great, you’re already aligned on controls—retainer operationalizes that alignment with predictable scope."

## 3) Conversation framework (Sprint exit → expansion close)

## Step 1: Re-anchor outcome
- "In Sprint we diagnosed X, with expected impact on [metric]. What outcome had the highest business value so far?"

## Step 2: Convert need to system
- "The next gap is recurrence control, not one-time fixes. Retainer does that through recurring scoring and governance cadence."

## Step 3: Link to cost of drift
- "Without recurring maturity checks, we know confidence decays from process drift, not from AI model quality alone."

## Step 4: Present options (showing clear line-item tradeoff)
- Choose tier by evidence maturity and number of systems.

## Step 5: Ask for directional commitment
- "If this helps reduce rework and makes reporting routine, would you prefer to start with one tier and scale at month 2?"

## Step 6: Create close path
- Confirm start date, success metric, and what ‘keep’ vs ‘pause’ means.

---

## 4) Offer options (Continuous Maturity retainer)

### Tier 1: Starter — $3,000/mo (1–2 priority workflows)
- Monthly rescoring for 1–2 mission-critical workflows
- Monthly evidence confidence check
- One KPI progress review + roadmap refresh
- Ops checklist and escalation template
- Best for: teams with initial sprint momentum but limited bandwidth

### Tier 2: Growth — $7,000/mo (3–5 workflows or cross-team governance)
- Biweekly score updates
- Drift detection alerts + remediation recommendations
- Monthly exec-ready scorecard + risk register summary
- Quarterly strategy review with engineering/security representation
- Best for: teams moving into multi-workflow operations

### Tier 3: Scale — $12,000–$15,000/mo (6+ workflows / enterprise governance)
- Weekly operating metrics and change monitoring
- Governance sprint support + retainer advisory sessions
- Quarterly benchmark review against peer baselines
- SLA-style escalation protocol and priority issue triage
- Best for: high-volume AI footprint, regulated environments, board visibility

**All tiers include:**
- Clear scope and exclusions
- Baseline reset and cadence plan
- Shared score interpretation + owner map
- Optional executive readout add-on as scope addendum

## 5) Objection handling for expansion

### Objection: "We should wait and see after this Sprint."
- **Acknowledge:** "That’s reasonable; we should avoid unnecessary spend."
- **Reframe:** "The decision is not about replacing your team—it's about preventing the diagnostic gains from degrading."
- **Evidence:** "Most teams with recurring changes lose signal within 4–8 weeks without cadence."
- **Close attempt:** "Can we start with 1 month Starter and review extension metrics before month 2?"

### Objection: "Retainer is too expensive."
- **Acknowledge:** "I understand—budget discipline matters."
- **Reframe:** "Without retainer, you may repurchase this intelligence through ad hoc effort at higher internal cost."
- **Evidence:** "Recurring support and repeated re-prioritization without a consistent cadence usually costs more than subscriptionized maturity checks."
- **Close attempt:** "Would one lighter tier that starts at $3k be useful now?"

### Objection: "Need CFO approval for ongoing spend."
- **Acknowledge:** "Exactly right; recurring spend needs commercial clarity."
- **Reframe:** "I can convert this into a staged financial plan with explicit monthly outcomes."
- **Evidence:** "We can set a clear value floor: each cycle produces scoring updates, decisions, and documented risk reduction priorities."
- **Close attempt:** "Would a 30-day Starter with a predefined continuation review be acceptable to finance?"

### Objection: "We can do compliance/QA internally."
- **Acknowledge:** "Perfectly valid and often ideal."
- **Reframe:** "Then retainer should act as a guardrail and acceleration mechanism for internal teams, not replacement."
- **Evidence:** "Retainer keeps evidence discipline and readiness cadence consistent, especially after initial fixes."
- **Close attempt:** "Would a co-managed Growth tier with clear roles help your internal team own execution while we keep cadence?"

### Objection: "No urgency anymore."
- **Acknowledge:** "Understood."
- **Reframe:** "No urgency today can become hidden risk tomorrow when priorities shift."
- **Evidence:** "The sprint output already created baselines and commitments; retaining them increases execution reliability."
- **Close attempt:** "Shall we set a deferred start with a fixed review date so this doesn’t fall off the radar?"

## 6) Expansion pipeline metrics (what makes upsell natural)

Use these to trigger proactive conversation:
1. **Sprint completion scorecard delivered on time** (target: all 4 deliverables on Day 5)
2. **Top gap ownership adherence** (≥70% assigned owners update progress by Day 30)
3. **Recurring escalation count** (2+ unresolved high-severity escalation in 30 days)
4. **Confidence decay** (key indicators drop or drift > threshold)
5. **Leadership reporting requests** (monthly leadership review now becoming a routine need)
6. **Reuse of sprint artifacts** (roadmap used in 2+ cross-functional syncs)

If at least 3 of 6 metrics trend upward, classify as **Expansion-Ready** and include retainer framework in next success review.

---

## 7) Standard retainer proposal path (decision meeting)

**Meeting frame (20 min):**
1. 3-min result recap (what changed since sprint)
2. 7-min drift risk explanation (what may degrade)
3. 5-min retainer tier match to their operating model
4. 3-min commercial confirmation and path

**Decision close attempt template:**
"If we agree that the Sprint outcome is valuable and implementation has to stay consistent across teams, would you like me to put the [Starter/Growth/Scale] option on hold for [date] with a 1-month trial cadence?"

---

## 8) KPI impact on team
- **Primary KPI:** sprint→retainer conversion rate
- **Secondary KPI:** average time from sprint completion to retainer proposal
- **Secondary KPI:** renewal/rollover intent score from week-2 and week-6 check-ins
- **Risk KPI:** number of stalled expansions with no timeline decision
