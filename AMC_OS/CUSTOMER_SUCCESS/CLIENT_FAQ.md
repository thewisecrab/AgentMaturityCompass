# CLIENT FAQ
## Compass Sprint Customer FAQ (Top 20)

## Pre-sale

1) **What is AMC?**  
AMC is the *AI Maturity Compass* program—commonly delivered as a 5-day Compass Sprint. It gives a structured assessment of your production AI systems, an evidence-based maturity score, and a practical 90-day roadmap.

2) **Who is this for?**  
Teams with production AI use that want governance and scaling clarity: usually SMB/mid-market companies with AI workflows already in use and at least one named owner.

3) **How is scoring done?**  
By a documented framework across maturity dimensions. Each control gets a level score only when supported by evidence or marked as a defined gap when evidence is missing.

4) **What is a realistic timeline?**  
Standard execution is 5 business days with a formal readout on Day 5.

5) **Do you guarantee score increases?**  
No. We provide a realistic baseline and recommendations; we do not guarantee exact numeric improvements.

## Onboarding

6) **What do we need ready before Day 1?**  
Signed SOW/NDA/payment, stakeholder list, AI architecture summary, agent inventory, and required evidence (for example: system/config docs, monitoring details, incident log, access-control policy, and success KPIs).

7) **How much prep time does the client need?**  
Typically 2–5 days of document collection plus Day 1 kickoff. The sprint itself is 5 days.

8) **Who should attend Day 1 and interviews?**  
At minimum sponsor + 2–3 SMEs. We recommend AI/ML lead, security/compliance lead, and operations/platform lead.

9) **What if we cannot provide a required artifact on time?**  
We proceed with what is available and explicitly mark gaps. Missing items reduce confidence and are shown in the findings.

10) **How long are interviews and how many are needed?**  
Each interview is ~45 minutes. At least 3 completed sessions are needed to proceed with stable scoring.

## During the sprint (Day 1–5)

11) **How are scores calculated day-by-day?**  
Day 1–2 focus on evidence intake and interviews, Day 3 scoring with conservative handling of unsupported claims, Day 4 roadmap construction, Day 5 readout. Scores are computed from control-level evidence coverage and validated by QA.

12) **What counts as valid evidence?**  
Artifacts and records with source ownership: prompts/configs, architecture docs, dashboards, incident tickets, policies, runbooks, cost records, evaluation/test docs, and interview notes.

13) **Do interview statements count the same as documents?**  
No. Documents and logs are stronger evidence. Interview input is used as contextual support and is scored conservatively unless verified.

14) **What if a scoring result looks too harsh or wrong?**  
We review disputes against source evidence and can adjust only with verifiable rationale and QA alignment.

15) **Can we ask for extra scope during the sprint?**  
Only if it is a small clarification. New scope requests are captured for a post-sprint phase unless formally re-scoped.

## Post-sprint (Day 6+) 

16) **What happens right after readout?**  
Client receives deck, roadmap, one-pager summary, and next-step options. A post-readout follow-up is sent within 2 hours of readout completion.

17) **How do we track improvement after Day 5?**  
Track roadmap execution on owner/date checkpoints (30/60/90 days), plus client pulse and follow-up check-ins.

18) **Is there a support process if we need help implementing?**  
Yes. Follow-on support options are discussed and can be proposed as separate retainers or implementation support.

19) **How do we collect quick feedback?**  
A 5-question survey is sent on Day 6 (one day after readout), and NPS is used to trigger referral/recovery workflows.

20) **How do we continue relationship momentum?**  
We use measurable checkpoints and communication cadence (including day-6 feedback and 30-day review) to keep execution moving and align on priorities.

## Scope and honesty notes

- The sprint gives a current-state maturity diagnosis, not a compliance certification.
- If evidence quality is limited, scores include confidence flags and explicit assumptions.
- Methodology, timeline, and outputs follow the published SOP and do not change without re-scope.
